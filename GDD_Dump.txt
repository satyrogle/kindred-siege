1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY 
 
The Elevator Pitch 
A Lovecraftian auto-battler where you command AI-driven units against eldritch horrors 
that remember you, adapt to your strategies, and come back stronger. Configure your army 
with Pre-Built Gambits, issue Tactical Directives mid-battle, and manage your units’ 
psychological resilience as sanity degrades under cosmic horror. A percentage of every sale 
and all Charity DLC revenue goes directly to a UK mental health charity. 
 
Working Title: KINDRED SIEGE: THE DROWNED CITY 
Genre: Lovecraftian Auto-Battler with Procedural Rivalry and Psychological Simulation 
Platform: PC (Steam) — keyboard/mouse, Steam Deck compatible 
Price: £14.99–£19.99 (premium, no microtransactions, no ads, no loot boxes) 
Engine: Unity 2022 LTS (C#) 
Developer: Solo developer — MSc Digital Games (Brunel University London) 
Target Audience: Fans of Darkest Dungeon, Slay the Spire, Shadow of Mordor, and cosmic

1.1 The Three Design Pillars 
Every design decision in Kindred Siege flows from three interconnected systems. Each pillar is a 
standalone innovation; together they create emergent gameplay no existing game offers. 
 
Pillar 1: AI Behaviour. Units fight autonomously using behaviour trees. Players don’t control 
units directly in combat —  they shape AI beforehand with Pre-Built Gambits and intervene during 
battle with limited Tactical Directives. The AI is the gameplay, not a hidden system. 
Pillar 2: Psychological Simulation. Every unit has a Sanity stat that degrades under stress and 
recovers through care. Low sanity causes hesitation, afflictions, and permanent breakdown. 
High-comprehension units suffer more from horror. Trauma creates phobias linked to specific 
rivals. This directly extends the developer’s MSc thesis on psychological resilience in life 
simulations. 
Pillar 3: The Rivalry Engine. Enemy leaders are procedurally generated with names, traits, 
weaknesses, and persistent memory. They remember your strategies, develop grudges, get 
promoted when they beat you, and can return from death as Undying horrors. Their rank 
determines Dread Power and Horror Rating. This creates emergent narrative without hand-
authored story.

2. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP 
 
The game follows a Darkest Dungeon-inspired expedition cycle: prepare at the Hamlet, choose a 
path, survive the auto-battle, return with rewards or losses, and rebuild. The entire campaign is a 
roguelite structure of 15–20 expeditions culminating in an Arch-Rival confrontation. 
 
2.1 The Expedition Cycle (One Loop) 
1. HAMLET PHASE: Recruit and heal units at the Barracks and Sanatorium. Manage Pre-
Built Gambit loadouts at the Archive. Review the Rivalry Board at the War Table. Spend 
Talent Points on veteran units. Check the Mercy Ledger at the Shrine. 
2. EXPEDITION MAP (Lighthouse): Choose from 2–3 paths. Each path shows: the 
encounter type, the active Reality Mutation(s), the rival you’ll face (with visible traits and 
rank), and the reward. Risk vs reward — harder paths yield better loot. 
3. PREPARATION PHASE: Select 4–6 units. Assign 2 Pre-Built Gambits per unit from their 
class dropdown. Review the mutation and rival information. Confirm and deploy. 
4. AUTO-BATTLE PHASE: Units deploy on a grid and fight autonomously. Player watches 
with speed controls (0.5x, 1x, 2x, 4x, pause). Sanity drains from combat events, Horror 
Rating, and prolonged fighting. Player spends Tactical Directive points (5 base) to 
intervene at critical moments. 
5. THE MERCY DECISION: When a unit reaches 0 HP, battle pauses. The player sees the 
unit’s name, class, expedition history, and traits. Spend a Mercy Token to revive them 
(30% HP, +15 sanity) or let them die permanently. 
6. POST-BATTLE: Rivalry Engine updates — rivals remember outcomes, gain traits, get 
promoted or scarred. Units gain XP and stress. Loot earned. Fatigue applied to deployed 
units. Return to Hamlet. 
 
Design Principle 
The player’s skill is expressed BEFORE and AROUND the battle — team selection, Gambit 
configuration, path choice, and Directive timing — not through direct unit control. 
The battle is the consequence of preparation. This is what makes it an auto-battler 
with strategic depth rather than a passive spectacle. 
 
2.2 Campaign Structure 
• Each run lasts 15–20 expeditions across 4–5 Districts, culminating in an Arch-Rival boss. 
• Permadeath: when units die, they’re gone forever. Veterans with invested Talent Points 
are devastating to lose. 
• Meta-progression between runs: Mythos Exposure carries forward, unlocking powerful 
Gambits and harder rivals. 
• Certain Undying rivals can persist across runs —  long-term emergent antagonists.

4. PLAYER INTERACTION: GAMBITS & DIRECTIVES 
 
These two systems replace the card system from earlier GDD versions. Pre-Built Gambits handle 
pre-battle AI configuration. Tactical Directives handle during-battle intervention. Together they 
give the player meaningful agency before AND during combat without breaking the auto-battler 
format. 
 
4.1 Pre-Built Gambits 
Before each expedition, the player assigns 2 Pre-Built Gambits to each unit via a simple 
dropdown menu. Each Gambit is a named, pre-packaged behaviour tree branch that injects at 
the top of the unit’s AI priority list. 
 
How It Works Under the Hood 
• Each Gambit is a C# method returning a BTNode (Sequence or Selector). 
• When the player selects a Gambit, the code takes that pre-built BT branch and injects it 
at Priority 1 or 2 in the unit’s behaviour tree. 
• The unit’s default class behaviour remains as a fallback below the Gambit priorities. 
• No visual node editor, no drag-and-drop — just a dropdown menu calling existing 
BattleActions.cs nodes. 
• Buildable in 1 day using existing architecture.

5.3 The Comprehension Stat 
Not all units process horror equally. Comprehension is a per-class float multiplier applied to ALL 
eldritch-source sanity damage. High-Comprehension units understand the horror — and pay the 
price. Low-Comprehension units are too simple to be afraid. 
 
Class Comprehension Rationale 
Warden 0.6 (Low) Simple soldier. Doesn’t think deeply. Reduced horror 
impact. 
Marksman 0.8 (Medium) Focused on targets, not cosmic implications. 
Occultist 1.3 (High) Understands the Mythos. Pays the price. 
Berserker 0.5 (Very Low) Pure instinct. Barely registers what they’re fighting. 
Investigator 1.5 (Very High) Actively comprehends everything. Maximum 
vulnerability. 
Shadow 0.7 (Low-Med) Operates on instinct and training. 
Herald 1.1 (High) Empathic awareness extends to the cosmic. 
Vessel 1.0 (Baseline) Standard. Already doomed by other means. 
 
Thesis Connection: Cognitive Appraisal 
Comprehension models the psychological concept of “appraisal” —  how individuals evaluate 
the significance of a stressful event. Research shows that cognitive appraisal is a stronger 
predictor of PTSD than the event itself. High-Comprehension units appraise horror more 
accurately and therefore suffer more — mirroring the clinical finding that awareness amplifies 
trauma. 
 
5.4 Forbidden Knowledge Trade-off 
Every time an Investigator uses the AnalyseTarget action to reveal a rival’s weakness, their 
MaxSanity permanently decreases by 2–3 points. This reduction persists across the entire 
campaign and cannot be healed. 
• Cap: maximum reduction of 40 points (MaxSanity cannot drop below 60% of base). 
• Veteran Investigators (5+ expeditions) lose 1 point less per analysis. 
• Creates a depreciating-asset dynamic: the Investigator becomes more valuable for intel 
but more psychologically fragile with each use. 
• At some point, the player must retire the Investigator or accept the risk of losing them to 
madness.

Re-exposing a phobic unit to their trigger mirrors exposure therapy. 
If they survive and gain a Virtue, the game models successful therapeutic outcome. 
If they break further, it models re-traumatisation. Both are psychologically authentic.

Rival taunts are stat contests, not flat damage. An Overlord taunting a Broken unit is devastating.

Unit Resistance = (Current Sanity / 10) + Comprehension Modifier 
Sanity Damage = max(0, Dread Power - Resistance) × 2 
 
Damage > 20: unit gains 3-second Hesitation Lock (cannot act)

7. REALITY MUTATIONS 
 
Procedural rule changes that alter fundamental game mechanics per battle. Mutations are 
presented on the Expedition Map at the Lighthouse, allowing the player to evaluate risk vs 
reward before choosing a path. 
 
7.1 Mutation Stacking 
• Initiate (Mythos 0–10): 1 mutation per battle 
• Acolyte (11–25): 1–2 mutations 
• Scholar (26–45): 2 mutations 
• Adept (46–70): 2–3 mutations 
• Seer (71+): 3 mutations, one hidden until battle starts 
 
7.2 Four Mutation Families 
 
Tide Mutations (Movement/Spatial) 
• “THE DEEP CALLS” — All units pulled toward grid centre (1 unit/tick) 
• “CURRENTS SHIFT” — Every 15 sec, all unit positions shuffle by 1–2 cells 
• “DROWNED GROUND” — Bottom 2 rows deal 3 sanity damage/tick 
• “RISING WATERS” — Playable grid shrinks by 1 row per side every 30 sec 
 
Mind Mutations (Sanity/Psychology) 
• “FEAR IS POWER” — Sanity damage converts to +damage bonus for 5 sec 
• “MADNESS SPREADS” — When a unit gains an Affliction, a random ally also gains one 
• “CLARITY IN PAIN” — Units below 25 sanity have 0% hesitation (the broken become 
focused) 
• “MEMORIES BLEED” — Veterans start with sanity equal to their lowest from previous 
expedition 
 
Flesh Mutations (Combat/HP) 
• “PAIN IS SHARED” — 30% of damage dealt also hits nearest ally 
• “DEATH FEEDS LIFE” — When a unit dies, all survivors heal 20% HP 
• “IRON BLOOD” — Armour doubled, healing halved 
• “THE WOUNDED STRIKE HARDER” — Units below 50% HP deal +40% damage 
 
Void Mutations (Rivalry/Existential) 
• “THE RIVAL KNOWS” — Rival reads your first Gambit slot and counters it (it fails) Kindred Siege: The Drowned City  |  GDD v4.0 Definitive Edition

• “NAMES HAVE POWER” — Units with phobias matching rival traits take 3x sanity

• “DEATH IS UNDONE” — Defeated enemies have 25% chance to respawn at 50% HP 
• “THE WATCHER SEES” — Tactical Directives cost double points 
 
7.3 Expedition Map (Risk vs Reward) 
At the Lighthouse, the player sees 2–3 paths. Each shows the mutation(s), the rival, the 
encounter type, and the reward. Harder paths yield better loot. Players evaluate the mutation-
rival combination to find paths that suit their team. 
• Example: Path A shows “FEAR IS POWER” + a rival with Ambusher trait (dangerous 
combo). Reward: Rare Gambit unlock. 
• Path B shows “IRON BLOOD” + a Fearful rival (manageable). Reward: Standard 
resources. 
• The player running a Berserker-heavy team might take Path A because Berserkers thrive 
on taking damage. 
 
7.4 Rival Resonance 
Mutations interact with rival traits to create unique tactical puzzles. The system checks if the 
active mutation amplifies any of the rival’s traits: 
• “BLOOD IN THE WATER” (all units move 50% faster) + Ambusher rival = rival crosses 
the map instantly 
• “FEAR IS POWER” + rival with Horror Rating 4 = your units take massive sanity damage 
that converts to a damage bonus, creating a race between psychological breakdown and 
killing power 
• The procedural mutation + procedural rival = a unique combat puzzle every battle 
 
7.5 Arch-Rival Domain Expansion 
At Mythos Exposure 100+, the Arch-Rival’s specific Reality Mutation is active across the ENTIRE 
game — every battle, not just encounters with the Arch-Rival. The player can’t avoid the 
mutation; they must hunt down and kill the Arch-Rival to break it. 
• Example: Undying Overlord projects “DEATH IS MADNESS” globally. Every unit that dies 
in any battle goes Lost instead, causing -20 sanity to everyone. 
• This forces the player to build a team specifically designed to hunt the Arch-Rival and 
end their domain.

9. TALENT TREE 
 
Each unit class has 10 talent nodes in two branches of 5. Units earn 1 talent point per survived 
expedition. A veteran with 8 expeditions has 8 points to spend across both branches. 
 
9.1 Branch Structure (Warden Example) 
Resolve Path (Psychological Durability) 
• Node 1: +10 max sanity 
• Node 2: Comprehension reduced by 0.1 
• Node 3: Immune to first Affliction roll per battle 
• Node 4: Ally death sanity loss halved (-7 instead of -15) 
• Node 5: Passive aura — nearby allies take 25% less Horror Rating drain 
 
Frontline Path (Combat Power) 
• Node 1: +15% HP 
• Node 2: +10% damage 
• Node 3: Armour blocks first sanity hit too (absorbs 5 sanity damage, once per battle) 
• Node 4: Taunt — enemies prioritise this unit for 5 seconds at battle start 
• Node 5: Heroic Sacrifice — on death, allies gain +10 sanity instead of losing -15 
 
Why This Matters 
Talent Trees make veteran units dramatically more valuable than fresh recruits. 
This raises the stakes for permadeath and the Mercy Token decision. 
A unit with 8 invested talent points is agonising to lose. 
Each path reflects Pillar 2: Resolve Path builds psychological resilience, 
Frontline Path builds combat capability. The choice mirrors the thesis question: 
does strength come from endurance or from power?

11. EMERGENT GAMEPLAY SYSTEMS 
 
11.1 Unit Bonds 
Units that survive 3+ expeditions together become Bonded. This is a counter, not a scripted 
event. 
• Bonded units: +5 sanity when within range of each other during battle. 
• If one Bonded partner dies: survivor takes -25 sanity instead of -15 (the loss hits harder). 
• Creates dilemmas: send both (Bond bonus but risk cascade) or split them (safe but 
weaker). 
 
11.2 Rivals Targeting Low-Sanity Units 
Rivals with the Tactical trait actively prioritise low-sanity player units. They know the broken ones 
will flee or attack allies. This connects Pillar 3 (rivalry AI) to Pillar 2 (psychological state) — your 
mental weakness becomes a tactical vulnerability. 
 
11.3 Event Cascades 
The most memorable moments are chain reactions across systems: 
13. Ally dies → teammates lose -15 sanity 
14. One teammate drops below 50 → Affliction roll → gains Paranoid 
15. Paranoid unit attacks ally → that ally takes damage 
16. Damaged ally is a Herald → empathic trait = double sanity loss 
17. Herald drops below 50 → Virtue roll → gains Courageous 
18. Courageous aura restores sanity to the Paranoid unit 
19. Paranoid unit stabilises and stops attacking allies 
Seven systems interacting. No scripted sequence. Pure emergence. 
 
11.4 Fatigue System 
Units deployed on consecutive expeditions gain Fatigue: -10% all stats per consecutive 
deployment. Two consecutive = -20%. Three = -30% and forced rest. This prevents “best team 
every time” and forces roster rotation.

14. THE MERCY MECHANIC & CHARITY MODEL 
 
No ads. No pay-to-win. The charity model works through three revenue streams on Steam. 
 
14.1 Mercy Tokens (In-Game) 
• Rare resource earned through gameplay: Vessel survival, Shrine upgrades, Mercy 
Cards, Arch-Rival defeats. 
• When a unit hits 0 HP, battle pauses. Player sees unit’s full history. 
• Spend 1 Mercy Token: unit revives at 30% HP with +15 sanity. 
• No token: unit dies permanently. 
• Mercy Ledger on main menu tracks total lives saved across all playthroughs. 
 
14.2 Charity Revenue Streams 
Stream 1: Base Game Donation 
• 10–15% of every sale donated to partnered UK mental health charity. 
• At £14.99 with 10% after Steam’s 30% cut: ~£1.05 per sale. 
 
Stream 2: Charity DLC Packs (£2.99–£4.99) 
• Cosmetic DLC where 100% of developer revenue goes to charity. 
• “The Mercy Collection” — skins, Hamlet themes, card backs. 
• Clearly labelled: “All proceeds donated to [Charity Name].” 
 
Stream 3: Community Milestones 
• Global Mercy Ledger: all players’ token usage aggregated. 
• Milestones (100k lives saved) trigger bonus donations and in-game events. 
• Creates press moments: “Kindred Siege players save 100,000 lives, donating £1,000 to 
Mind.”

THE THREE-PILLAR TEST 
 
Before building ANY feature, ask: does this serve AI Behaviour (Pillar 1), Psychological 
Simulation (Pillar 2), or the Rivalry Engine (Pillar 3)? 
 
• If it serves one pillar: build it. 
• If it connects two pillars: prioritise it. 
• If it connects all three: it’s the most valuable feature in the game. 
• If it serves no pillar: it’s scope creep. Cut it. 
 
The Innovation Story 
Pillar 1 (AI) drives combat. Gambits modify behaviour trees. Comprehension differentiates 
classes. 
Pillar 2 (Psych) tracks mental state. Phobias link to specific trauma. Knowledge has a cost. 
Pillar 3 (Rivalry) drives escalation. Rivals cause dread, horror, phobias, and exposure. 
 
No pillar operates in isolation. Every battle touches all three. 
That interconnection IS the innovation. That is what makes this game unique. 
That is the story the visa application tells.
